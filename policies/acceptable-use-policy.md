## 1. Purpose
This Acceptable Use Policy defines the acceptable and unacceptable uses of **[ORGANIZATION NAME]** information systems, technology resources, and network infrastructure. It protects the organisation, its employees, and third parties from legal liability, reputational damage, and information security incidents.

**All employees must read, understand, and sign the acknowledgement form prior to system access.**

---

## 2. Permitted Uses

### 2.1 Business Use
All technology resources are provided for legitimate business purposes:
- Performing job-related tasks and communications
- Accessing business-approved applications and services
- Participating in approved training and development activities
- Reasonable personal use that does not impede business operations or consume significant resources

### 2.2 Personal Use Guidelines
Personal use is permitted if it is:
- Infrequent and minimal in duration
- Does not involve prohibited activities (Section 3)
- Does not consume significant bandwidth or storage
- Does not expose the organisation to liability

---

## 3. Prohibited Uses

### 3.1 Security Violations (Zero Tolerance)
- Attempting to gain unauthorised access to any system, network, or data
- Using another person's credentials or sharing your own
- Installing unauthorised software, tools, or scripts
- Disabling or bypassing security controls (antivirus, DLP, firewalls)
- Conducting vulnerability scans or penetration tests without written authorisation
- Storing sensitive data in unapproved locations (personal cloud, USB without encryption)

### 3.2 Legal Violations
- Downloading, distributing, or possessing copyright-infringing material
- Accessing, distributing, or storing illegal content of any kind
- Using organisation systems for financial fraud, identity theft, or money laundering
- Violating privacy laws (GDPR, DPDPA, HIPAA, CCPA) — mishandling personal data
- Defamation or harassment via electronic communications

### 3.3 Business-Harmful Activities
- Sending unsolicited bulk email (spam) from any organisation account
- Accessing or forwarding confidential information to unauthorised parties
- Using organisation resources for personal business ventures
- Mining cryptocurrency using organisation computing resources
- Activities that damage the organisation's reputation

---

## 4. Monitoring & Privacy

**[ORGANIZATION NAME]** reserves the right to monitor all activity on organisation-owned systems and networks. Users have **no expectation of privacy** when using organisation resources.

Monitoring may include:
- Email content and metadata
- Web browsing history (corporate network and VPN)
- Application usage logs
- Keyboard and screen activity on managed devices
- File transfers and removable media usage

*Monitoring is conducted in accordance with applicable law and with appropriate oversight.*

---

## 5. Violations & Consequences

| Violation | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| Minor (first occurrence) | Written warning + mandatory retraining |
| Moderate | Formal disciplinary action + restricted access |
| Serious | Termination + potential legal action |
| Criminal | Immediate termination + law enforcement referral |
